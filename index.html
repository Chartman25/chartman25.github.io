Casey Hartman

M.Sc. Astrophysics · Université de Genève

I am a master's student in astrophysics at the Université de Genève, working on problems at the intersection of stellar physics and exoplanet science. My research focuses on stellar surface distortions, circumbinary planet detection, and protoplanetary disk evolution in binary systems.

I completed my B.S. in Astrophysics (Magna Cum Laude) at Tufts University in 2025, where I also held positions in the Department of Physics and Astronomy and led the Students for the Exploration and Development of Space (SEDS).
