Current Projects

Distortions in the Stellar Surface

Observatoire de Genève · Prof. Vincent Bourrier · Jun. 2025 – Present

Updating the ANTARESS workflow (Bourrier et al. 2024) to process high-resolution spectroscopy datasets. Work includes deriving a correction term for the stellar surface radial velocity equation to account for oblateness and tidal bulging, and modeling gravity darkening due to tides induced by a companion planet.

TESS Search for Transiting Circumbinary Planets

Tufts Dept. of Physics & Astronomy · Prof. David Martin · Sept. 2024 – Present

Modified the circumbinary planet search algorithm STANLEY (Martin et al. 2021) to search TESS light curves for transiting planets. Repurposed the detrending method for TESS cadence and employed linear interpolation on the period vs. theta search grid, cutting compute time in half. Paper in prep for MNRAS (early 2026).

Past Projects

Modeling Circumstellar Disk Truncation in Binary Systems

Observatoire de Genève · Prof. Julia Venturini · Sept. 2025 – Dec. 2025

Derived an analytic function mapping binary eccentricity, separation, disk viscosity, and stellar masses to the truncation radii of protoplanetary disks. Analyzed planet embryo evolution using the Bern Model (Emsenhuber et al. 2021), examining mass growth, orbital migration, and pebble accretion under varying binary parameters.

Direct Dark Matter Detection — XENONnT

Weizmann Institute of Science · Ran Budnik's Lab · Jun. 2023 – Aug. 2023

Worked within the XENONnT international collaboration on direct dark matter detection. Calibrated a neutron generator and designed an experiment to characterize its flux at varying distances and angles. Analyzed experimental data in Python using statistical tests to validate detector signals.
